New infrastructure projects break ground across all nine districts

A summer of road resurfacing, sidewalk repairs, and stormwater upgrades — see what’s coming to your neighborhood and when.

CO City of Birmingham Public Works

Department of Public Works · June 22, 2026

Crews break ground on the first of this summer’s resurfacing projects downtown.

The City of Birmingham this week began the largest single season of neighborhood infrastructure work in recent memory, with crews breaking ground on projects in all nine council districts. The summer program pairs routine maintenance — repaving, sidewalk repair, and curb work — with longer-term investments in the city’s aging stormwater system.

“Every resident, in every part of the city, should be able to see and feel this investment on their own street,” the Mayor’s Office said in announcing the schedule. “That’s the standard we set, and that’s what this summer delivers.”

What’s included

The program is organized into three coordinated tracks so that streets are not torn up more than once:

  • Road resurfacing on more than 60 miles of city streets, prioritized by pavement-condition scores
  • Sidewalk and curb-ramp repairs to improve accessibility near schools, parks, and transit stops
  • Stormwater and drainage upgrades in neighborhoods with a history of flooding
  • Restriping and new signage once each segment is repaved
We sequenced the work so a street gets its drainage fixed, then repaved, then restriped — once. No coming back three times.

How to follow your street

Residents can look up their address on the city’s interactive map to see which projects are active nearby, view the expected start and end dates, and sign up for text alerts when work begins on their block. Temporary "no parking" signage will be posted at least 48 hours before crews arrive.

Questions about a specific project can be directed to 311, where staff can confirm timing and detours for any active work zone.
