Neighborhood grant applications now open

Community associations can apply for beautification, safety, and small-business support grants through the end of the quarter.

OO Office of Community Development

City of Birmingham · June 20, 2026

Neighborhood associations across the city are invited to apply.

The City of Birmingham has opened applications for its neighborhood grant program, making funds available to community associations for projects that improve safety, appearance, and economic vitality at the block level.

The program is designed to put decision-making in the hands of the residents who know their neighborhoods best, with a streamlined application meant to be accessible to volunteer-run associations.

What grants can fund

  • Beautification — landscaping, murals, gateway signage, and cleanups
  • Safety — lighting improvements, crosswalks, and traffic-calming
  • Small-business support — façade improvements and pop-up markets
  • Community events that bring neighbors together
The best ideas for a neighborhood almost always come from inside it. This program funds those ideas.

Deadlines & how to apply

Applications are open now through the end of the quarter. Each recognized neighborhood association may submit one primary request per cycle. City staff are available to help associations scope projects and complete the application; contact 311 to be connected with a community-development liaison.
