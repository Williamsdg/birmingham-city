Mayor announces expanded summer youth programs

Thousands of paid internships and free recreation programs open to Birmingham students across community centers this season.

OO Office of the Mayor

City of Birmingham · June 21, 2026

Summer programming returns to parks and community centers citywide.

The Mayor’s Office today announced a significant expansion of the city’s summer youth programming, adding paid internships, free recreation, and meal access for thousands of Birmingham students between the close of the school year and the start of fall.

The expansion builds on the city’s existing youth-employment initiative, which places high-school and college-age residents in paid roles across city departments, local nonprofits, and partner employers.

What’s new this year

  • More paid internship slots across city departments and partner organizations
  • Free programming at every community center — sports, arts, STEM, and mentorship
  • Expanded summer-meal sites so no participating child goes without lunch
  • Extended evening hours at select recreation centers
When we invest in our young people in the summer, we’re investing in the Birmingham they’ll lead.

How to sign up

Families can register at any community center or online through the Parks & Recreation portal. Internship applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, and recreation programming is open to all Birmingham students at no cost.
